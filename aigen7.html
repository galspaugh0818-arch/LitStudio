Analysis: Kurzgesagt's Use of AI and Ethical Considerations

By Gregory Alspaugh • October 2025
Twitter

Overall, Kurzgesagt’s video is more good than bad, presenting a largely accurate and insightful look at the challenges AI poses to creativity, misinformation, and the attention economy. Their examples, such as AI confidently generating false Brown Dwarf facts, are vivid and instructive, and their ethical concerns about scraped human work are entirely justified. While the video sometimes leans into alarmist language, framing AI as the “death of the internet,” and takes an absolutist stance in slogans like “we will never make AI slop,” these exaggerations do not undermine the core logic of their argument. Importantly, Kurzgesagt’s own use of AI is practical and ethically sound: they employ it only as a tool for efficiency, such as organizing research, automating repetitive tasks, or streamlining production, while keeping human creativity, fact-checking, and final decisions central. This balance allows them to avoid producing AI slop while still benefiting from technological assistance, demonstrating a responsible and realistic approach. In sum, the video is highly informative and morally grounded, and their AI usage serves as a model for how creators can integrate technology without compromising quality or integrity.

Summary

Kurzgesagt provides an accessible overview of AI's potential harms and practical uses, framing concerns clearly while demonstrating a pragmatic approach to integrating AI in production.

Strengths

Clear examples, practical ethical stance, and transparent production practices. The video balances urgency with constructive recommendations.

Weaknesses

Occasional alarmist language and strong-sounding slogans that may oversimplify nuance for impact.

Recommendations

Adopt transparent sourcing, maintain human oversight for creative work, and use AI for efficiency rather than creative substitution.
